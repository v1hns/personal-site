ML Research Internship · Institute for Systems Biology · 2025

Computational Biology Modeling

Predictive machine learning models for HIV and preeclampsia diagnosis — developed at the Institute for Systems Biology under the Baliga Lab.

Overview

Conducted ML research at the Institute for Systems Biology (ISB) — one of the world's leading systems biology research centers, known for pioneering work in network-based approaches to disease and the development of P4 Medicine (predictive, preventive, personalized, participatory).

Working under the Baliga Lab, I developed predictive computational biology models targeting two critical health challenges: HIV diagnosis and preeclampsia — a leading cause of maternal mortality worldwide. The research focused on integrating multi-omic datasets to surface biomarker signatures that could enable earlier, more accurate diagnosis.

Research focus

HIV modeling: Applied supervised ML to multi-omic patient data to identify expression patterns predictive of HIV status and progression, with the goal of improving detection in low-resource clinical settings.

Preeclampsia modeling: Built regression and classification models on proteomic and transcriptomic data to identify early biomarker signatures of preeclampsia risk — a condition where early detection is critical but currently difficult.
