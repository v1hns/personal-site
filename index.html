a small book about what one person can build before they're supposed to be able to. presonance, oncoscan, a brain-stroke model that won a science fair, and a long line of things broken on purpose and rebuilt until they held. the method never changes: build it, break it, retrain it. the rest is just water and air.
acknowledgements
thank you to the people who took a teenager seriously before there was a reason to: the advisors at meta and georgia tech, and the founders who somehow always picked up the phone. to the researchers and clinicians who let me work on things that mattered, to the students and schools who trusted the uplift program, and to my family, who watched me start far too many things at once and never once told me to stop.
