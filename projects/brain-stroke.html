Brain Stroke Diagnostic Model

A 3D convolutional neural network for CT scan classification — predicting stroke susceptibility from volumetric medical imaging data.

🏆 1st Place — WA State Science & Engineering Fair

Overview

Developed a 3D convolutional neural network (3D CNN) to classify brain CT scans for stroke susceptibility. Unlike 2D models that analyze individual slices, the 3D architecture captures volumetric spatial relationships across the full scan — enabling more accurate diagnosis of ischemic and hemorrhagic stroke indicators.

The model was trained on a curated dataset of annotated CT scans and evaluated against radiologist-level benchmarks. The work was awarded 1st Place at the Washington State Science and Engineering Fair (WSSEF) — one of the most competitive regional STEM competitions in the country, feeding into international fairs.

Architecture

The model uses a custom 3D CNN pipeline with volumetric convolutions, batch normalization, and max-pooling across three spatial dimensions. A fully connected classifier head outputs susceptibility scores for multiple stroke classifications. Data augmentation techniques (random flipping, rotation, intensity jitter) were applied to improve generalization on limited medical imaging data.

Impact

Stroke is a leading cause of death and disability worldwide. Early, automated detection from CT imaging can dramatically reduce time-to-treatment. This model demonstrates that a 3D deep learning approach can meaningfully assist clinical triage — especially in under-resourced settings where specialized radiologists are unavailable.
